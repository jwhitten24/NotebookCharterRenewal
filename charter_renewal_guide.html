A step-by-step guide to using Google NotebookLM to prepare, draft, and organize your California charter renewal petition materials.

What is NotebookLM?

NotebookLM is a free AI research tool from Google. Think of it as a smart reading assistant: you upload your school's documents, and it reads them so you can ask questions, find gaps, and generate polished summaries — all grounded in your actual files.

This guide walks you through the exact workflow Troy Beyer at Literacy First Charter School (LFCS) used to prepare their 2026 SDCOE renewal — adapted here for charter schools working with Charter SELPA. No AI experience required.

Step 1

Set Up NotebookLM

Getting started takes about 5 minutes. All you need is a Google account.

  • 1

    Go to notebooklm.google.com

    Open a browser and navigate to notebooklm.google.com. Sign in with your school Google account (the same one you use for Gmail or Drive).

  • 2

    Create a New Notebook

    Click "New Notebook" in the top-right corner. Give it a clear name — for example: 2026 Charter Renewal — [Your School Name]. This notebook will hold all your source documents.

  • 3

    Understand the Interface

    The left panel is your Sources (documents you upload). The center is the Chat area where you type prompts. The right panel shows Artifacts — things NotebookLM has created for you.

  • 4

    Keep it to one notebook per renewal cycle

    Adding all your renewal documents to a single notebook lets the AI cross-reference everything at once — which is where it gets powerful.

🔑
Important: Use your official school Google account, not a personal one. This keeps your renewal materials within your school's data environment.
Step 2

Upload Source Documents

The quality of NotebookLM's output depends entirely on the quality of documents you provide. Here's what to upload.

You'll build a source library — a targeted collection of your school's historical data and your authorizer's current requirements. Think of it as giving the AI the same reading stack you'd give a new staff member joining your renewal team.

Document Type What to Include Why It Matters
Historical
Previous Charter Petition		 Your most recent approved charter petition (with all appendices) Establishes baseline — AI compares this against new requirements to find gaps
Procedural
Authorizer Guides		 Pre-renewal presentation, FAQs, submission instructions from your authorizer Sets the timeline, format rules (PDF vs. Excel), and public hearing expectations
Legal
Legislative Updates		 Documents listing new laws enacted since your last renewal (e.g., SB 707, SB 848, AB 285) Tells the AI which new California Ed Code sections must appear in your updated petition
Evaluation
Petition Review Matrix		 Your authorizer's scoring rubric (often a Word or PDF document) Lets AI map each petition element to the exact EC 47605 criteria you'll be scored on
Submission
Renewal Forms & Guides		 Any intake forms, spotlight presentation guides, or board presentation rules Captures executive leadership requirements and presentation structure rules
📂
Upload format tip: NotebookLM accepts PDFs, Google Docs, Google Slides, and web URLs. For best results, upload PDFs directly rather than scanned images. If your authorizer uses a SharePoint portal, download files first and then upload them to NotebookLM.
Step 3

Write Effective Prompts

A well-structured prompt is the key to getting charter-ready output — not generic summaries. Use the Role + Sources + Output formula.

🎭 Role

Tell NotebookLM to act as an expert. "Act as an experienced charter school administrator preparing for a 2026 renewal…"

📄 Sources

Name the specific uploaded documents to cross-reference. "Using the 2021 Charter Petition and the Legislative Changes document…"

📋 Output Format

Specify exactly what you want back: a checklist, a slide outline, a table, a bulleted report, draft language, etc.

🎯 Specificity

The more specific you are about California Ed Code sections, petition elements, or deadline dates, the more targeted the output.

Example: Identifying Legislative Compliance Gaps

Sample Prompt — Copy & Paste into NotebookLM

"Act as an expert charter school administrator preparing for our 2026 authorizer renewal. Using the uploaded 'Previous Charter Petition' and the 'Renewal Legislative Changes' documents, generate a tailored report identifying all new employee and student safety laws passed since our last renewal (such as SB 848 for child abuse prevention and SB 707 for teleconference rules). List the specific new legal requirements, identify which section of our current petition needs to be rewritten, and provide a bulleted checklist of updates we must make to be compliant."

Example: Gap Analysis for Academic Data

Sample Prompt — Academic Data Gaps

"Acting as a charter renewal specialist, review our uploaded petition and the authorizer's 2026 Petition Review Matrix. Identify every place where our current data section references outdated enrollment or test score figures. Create a table with three columns: (1) Petition Section, (2) What Is Currently Written, (3) What Data Source We Need to Pull (e.g., CALPADS, CAASPP, CDE Enrollment files). Format the output as a ready-to-share action list for our administrative team."

⚠️
Always verify AI output: NotebookLM grounds its responses in your uploaded documents, but you must have a qualified staff member or attorney review any legal language or Ed Code citations before including them in a submitted petition.
Step 4

Generate Renewal Artifacts

NotebookLM can produce three types of structured outputs — each useful at a different stage of your renewal process.

  • A

    Tailored Gap Reports

    Ask NotebookLM to compare your 2021 (or prior) petition against 2026 authorizer requirements. It will surface specific sections that need updating — for example, identifying that your governance section needs SB 707 teleconference language added before July 1, 2026.

  • B

    Structured Outlines & Mind Maps

    Prompt it to untangle complex submission procedures. NotebookLM can synthesize your authorizer's requirements into a visual outline of the 15 required petition elements, organized by which documents need to be submitted in which format (PDF, Word, Excel, web form).

  • C

    Public Hearing Slide Deck Outlines

    Given that most authorizers allow exactly 15 minutes for school presentations, you can prompt NotebookLM to generate a slide-by-slide outline covering all required talking points: school overview, demographics vs. district averages, academic CAASPP data, and community connections. Export to PowerPoint format.

Submission rule: The latest file timestamp on your authorizer's portal becomes your official submission date. Submit all files at once and do not attempt to amend mid-process.
Step 5 · Final

Compliance Checklist

Check off each item as your team completes it. Your progress is saved in this session.

Phase 1 — Manuscript Finalization

  • Upload previous charter petition to NotebookLM notebook
  • Upload all authorizer procedural guides and FAQs
  • Upload Legislative Changes document (new laws since last renewal)
  • Upload Petition Review Matrix from authorizer
  • Upload renewal intake forms and submission instructions
  • Run gap analysis prompt — identify all outdated petition sections
  • Generate legislative compliance report (SB 707, SB 848, AB 285, etc.)
  • Draft updates to Governance section (teleconference disruption policy by July 1)
  • Update Element A / Health & Safety with SB 848 employee misconduct protocols
  • Pull current CALPADS and CDE enrollment data for data section

    • Phase 2 — Digital Compliance

  • Remediate all petition PDFs to WCAG 2.1 AA (add alt-text, fix reading order, check color contrast)
  • Remediate all PowerPoint/presentation files for accessibility
  • Generate clean petition (fully updated text) as accessible PDF
  • Generate redline version showing all substantive changes
  • Map all page numbers in the Review Matrix; sign completeness certificate

    • Phase 3 — Submission

  • Complete authorizer's online renewal form (facilities, SELPA status, related entities)
  • Prepare Public Hearing slide deck (15-minute format, accessible PPT, no audio/video)
  • Upload all files to authorizer's submission portal in one session
  • Confirm submission timestamp and retain confirmation record
